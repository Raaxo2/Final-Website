Chi Modu

Chi Modu was born on July 7, 1966 in Aronbizoug, Nigeria. Then in 1969, Modu and his family moved to the United States. In 1984, he graduated from the Lawrenceville School in Princeton in 1964 and then going to New Jersey earning his B.S. degree in economics from The State University of New Jersey, Rutgers in 1989.

Moduâ€™s photography has been featured in multiple exhibitions across the world. His work at these exhibitions was later compiled into a book that was published in 2016, titled, Tupac Shakur: UNCATEGORIZED, which features many of his iconic photographs of the rap artist from the 1990s.

His first working position as a photographer was with the African American newspaper the New York Amsterdam News in Harlem. He would soon behired by The Source magazine in 1991, where he worked as the director of photography. He would be chosen for multiple covers in which he worked with some of the biggest names in hip hop such as Tupac Shakur, Snoop Dogg, LL Cool J, Mary J. Blige and the Notorious B.I.G.
